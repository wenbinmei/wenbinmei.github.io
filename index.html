Biography

Wenbin Mei is currently a Postdoctoral Scholar in Dr. Jeffrey Ross-Ibarra’s lab at UC Davis. His work focuses on the population genetics of copy number variants and inversions in both maize and teosinte. His PhD dissertation work under the supervision of Dr. William (Brad) Barbazuk was to understand the complexity of alternative splicing landscape in maize, to develop methods that can identify evolutionary conserved alternative splicing events under functional constraints across grass genomes, and to find potential links between epigenetic regulation and alternative splicing.

Wenbin Mei has a broad range of interests in regulatory genomics and the evolution of plant genomes using combined approaches such as functional genomics, population genetics and quantitative genetics. His long-term research goal is to bridge the gap in the genotype-phenotype map, and to study how genetic variation responds and adapts to environmental perturbations by investigating “dark matter” of plant genomes such as functional regulatory elements, post-transcriptional gene regulations and structural variation.
